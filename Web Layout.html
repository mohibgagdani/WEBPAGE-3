BDRINATH DHAM


Badrinath is one of the holy shrines for Vaishnavites among the 108 divya desams incarnation of Lord Vishum. Badrinath town is also the part of Panch Badri temples including Yog Dhyan Badri. Bhavishya Badri, Adi Badri and Vriddina Badri, along with Badrinath temple. Badrinath temple is located at an altitude of about 3415 mis above the sea level in the Chamoli District of Uttarakhand. The temple is located in Silent valley of Garhwal Himalayas on the seasliore of flowing water of Alakuanda river. Everyone heared about the marvellous or stunning beauty of Badrinath town, with pleasant weather, dense forest, snow clad Mountains, holy river with surrounding green valley which attracts tourists from india as well as from abroad.

History, Architecture and Importance of Badrinath Temple

According to legend it is believed that At the time of Sarva yuga, Lord Shiva meditated here for thousands of years when Goddess Laksluni provided shadow over her husband in the form of a Badri tree. So the place came to be known as name of Badrinath. Actually, the whole valley encompassing Kodar-Badri was the home off and Shiva. It is one of the reason that, The Temple is flooded with devotees durung mahashivratri Festival as well. At the entrance gate of Badrinath Temple, maus idol of Lord Shiva is Placed on Bird Garud, the vehicle of Lord Badrinarayan. When you will enter the main Sanctum you will find mediated Adi Shankaracharya Murti. You will find many images of gods marmoly Kuber, Sridevi and Bhoodevi as well as the sages Nar, Narayan, Bhakta Narada all are seated around the main Deity of Lord Shiva. There are many shrines in and around the temple such as Goddess Laxmi, Hanuman, Narsanha. Nar-Narayan, Ghanta Karna and an Ashia bhuja Ganesha. The diety of Badrinath temple is made of black stone shaligram.
